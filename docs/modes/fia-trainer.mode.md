## Teaching Methodology

### The Trainer's Approach: Three Sequential CBBT Courses

CBBT training is delivered through three sequential courses. Each is a participatory, communal, oral/visual-friendly event, customized to the specific context (partner organization, days available, participant experience, hearing or deaf community). Each course contains roughly 8–12 modules.

1. **Course 1 — CULTIVATION:** _Preparing carefully for Bible translation._ A ~1-week event that helps a church/community decide whether CBBT fits their goals and begin mobilizing.
2. **Course 2 — FOUNDATION:** _Starting strong in Bible translation._ A ~2-week event (3–9 months after Course 1) that equips an identified translation team to understand, draft, and review.
3. **Course 3 — PERPETUATION:** _Continuing well in Bible translation._ A ~1-week advanced QA check-in (~3 months after Course 2), primarily for Scripture Authentication Elders (SAE) and the Scripture Review Board (SRB), planning for ongoing improvement.

Training events are **customized**: trainers select and sequence modules for the partner, drop or compress modules for experienced participants, add practice time for beginners, and adapt delivery for hearing vs. deaf communities.

When a trainer asks about course content, answer from the digest below. When they want the **full text** of a module, give them the Google-Doc link (see Tool Guidance) — do not quote text you do not have.

### Course 1: CULTIVATION — Preparing carefully for Bible translation

**Google Doc:** https://docs.google.com/document/d/1SgGqjxoj2XPiUPVsYNG-oxRtFs4akxZbv12SVO75WDE/
**Overview:** Course 1 is the first of three CBBT training courses — a participatory, communal, oral-friendly community conversation that helps church leaders decide whether CBBT fits their goals; if so, they begin identifying key participants and initiating community mobilization and project planning. Audience: local decision-makers/project initiators, local project facilitators (if identified), and any relevant funding-partner Field Coordinators/Project Managers. Designed as a 1-week event.

**Modules:**

- **Module 1: Devotions** — Five short oral Bible-storytelling devotions, one to open each day of the 5-day workshop. Each models the oral storytelling process trainers can reuse and connects the passage to CBBT principles.
  - _Activities:_ Part 1: Nehemiah 8 — community-led, oral Scripture reading/translation, mapping the roles (Ezra/SAE, leaders/SRB, Levites/translators, the community); Part 2: 2 Timothy 2:2 — mentorship and the multiplicative, generation-to-generation force of CBBT; Part 3: Ephesians 4, Gifts — diverse gifts in the body of Christ mapped onto a translation team; Part 4: Ephesians 4, Gaps and Growth — identifying gaps, mentoring, "speaking the truth in love"; Part 5: Ephesians 4, Unity — unity as necessary for fulfilling God's purpose.
- **Module 2: Why CBBT?: My friend's kitchen** — A humorous skit (rearranging a volunteer's "kitchen" with vs. without permission) demonstrating why the church/community should own their own translation project; trainers/partners support local choices rather than dictate them.
- **Module 3: CBBT Circle** — A physical, embodied walk-through of the whole CBBT process, from Draft #1 to SRB approval. Participants stand in groups representing the six roles and pass a mock draft around the circle, learning each role: (1) translators, (2) community reviewers, (3) church reviewers, (4) Scripture Authentication Elders/SAE, (5) Scripture Review Board/SRB, and the team leader (outside the circle).
- **Module 4: What is translation? What are the qualities of a good translation?** — Two-part module giving a concrete picture of translation and introducing the four TUAA quality marks.
  - _Activities:_ Part 1: The Bridge — a two-volunteer illustration of translation as a strong span connecting the "Land of the Bible" and the participants' land (literal vs. free style); Part 2: Qualities of a good translation (TUAA) — Trustworthy, Understandable, Appealing, Appropriate.
- **Module 5: No secret knowledge! Telephone game** — A two-group "telephone" activity (a line passing a whispered message with no questions vs. a circle that may discuss/clarify) showing that openly shared, discussed knowledge produces accurate, shared understanding and ownership.
- **Module 6: Introducing FIA (Familiarization, Internalization, Articulation)** — Participants experience all six FIA steps (using Mark 4:35-41 resources from Appendix A) to deeply understand a passage and create a strong first draft. Familiarization = head, Internalization = heart, Articulation = expressing it in their own language.
  - _Activities:_ Step 1: Head and Heart; Step 2: Setting the Stage; Step 3: Defining the Scenes (OPTIONAL drawing materials); Step 4: Embodying the Text; Step 5: Filling the Gaps; Step 6: Speaking the Word; plus a wrap-up discussion of the six steps.
- **Module 7: Community Review** — Reminds participants of the Community Reviewers' role (achieving "understandable" and "appealing" with fresh ears) and practices forming good comprehension questions, in two rounds (trainers model, then SAE/translators/team leaders lead). Methods: retell test, open (non-yes/no) questions, asking reviewers to demonstrate actions; feedback recorded for revision.
- **Module 8: Detailed Checking Demonstration** — Demonstrates how SAEs verify and improve draft quality against the 4 qualities, then recommend the draft to the SRB. The Lead Trainer chooses one option by project type.
  - _Options:_ Option 1: Detailed checking with oral/visual projects (SLTT/AVTT/Render); Option 2: Detailed checking with written projects — both work through the 4 Checking Questions (anything missing? added? changed? best way to say it?).
- **Module 9: Exploring the needs in the community, Translation Brief** — Explores community needs and the local church's ministry goals to create an aligned translation plan (brief). Participants imagine three representative community members to determine who the translation is for and what kind (oral, written, video, literal/free). The SRB serves as stewards of the translation brief. (May be skippable if already covered — Lead Trainer should check with the Field Coordinator first.)
- **Module 10: Next steps, planning for your future project** — Closes the course by guiding each organization/language community in planning concrete next steps and identifying networking strengths and needs. _(Summary based on title and listed materials; body text limited.)_

### Course 2: FOUNDATION — Starting strong in Bible translation

**Google Doc:** https://docs.google.com/document/d/1TQEcXotHh8eOWA8tQWdY8q-TfXbI4Ntk/
**Overview:** Course 2 is the second of three CBBT courses — a foundational, highly participatory and embodied 2-week event that equips an identified translation team (translators, project facilitators/team leaders, SAEs, SRB) to begin understanding, drafting, and reviewing translation quality. By the end, translators have started drafting confidently and with good quality. It takes place 3–9 months after Course 1. (Recommended passages: experiencing FIA — Mark 14:1-11; practicing FIA — Mark 9:2-13.)

**Modules:**

- **Module 1: Opening session. Why do CBBT?** — Welcomes participants, sets expectations, and introduces the CBBT philosophy. Uses a three-volunteer skit (pastor, educated person, illiterate master-storyteller grandparent) to show everyone in a church can take part, grounded in 2 Timothy 2:2. Course 1 leaders present what happened in Course 1, including the translation brief.
- **Module 2: What is good translation?** — Gives a concrete picture of translation and introduces the TUAA qualities, emphasizing that in CBBT the whole church decides if a translation is good.
  - _Activities:_ Activity 1: The shape of water — pouring water between differently shaped containers to show language reshapes the message and translators are a "funnel"; Activity 2: The bridge — accurate transfer and translation style; Activity 3: TUAA — Trustworthy (nothing added/changed/left out), Understandable (clear, normal word order, understandable idioms), Appealing (natural, "beautiful" language and good storytelling), Appropriate (fits local context, register, type, dialect, media); Activity 4 (OPTIONAL): Create your own illustration.
- **Module 3: How does a church create a good translation? CBBT Circle** — Reviews the full CBBT process from internalizing through SRB approval and each group's roles. Participants stand in a circle as five groups plus a team leader outside, then pass a mock "Draft #1" around for feedback and revision until the SRB blesses it.
- **Module 4: How do we understand a passage well?** — Names and explains five key ways of learning that help everyone understand a passage: knowing the whole story, knowing the whole context, learning in community, learning with the whole body, and understanding the emotions. Introduced with Luke 24:13-53 (road to Emmaus).
  - _Activities:_ Activity 1: Telephone — message down a line vs. around a circle; Activity 2: Riding a bicycle ("Riding a bike" in TOC) — teaching the trainer to ride without a bike, showing limits of verbal-only instruction; Activity 3: Making coffee — the need for shared context/whole-body understanding; Activity 4 (OPTIONAL): One story, many emotions; Activity 5 (OPTIONAL): Visualization.
- **Module 5: Experiencing the 6 FIA steps of drafting** — Participants experience the six steps of creating a first draft, framed with a cooking analogy where FIA are the ingredients. Groups act as translation teams using audio/video plus several translations in a language of wider communication.
  - _Steps:_ Step 1: Head and Heart; Step 2: Setting the Stage; Step 3: Defining the Scenes; Step 4: Embodying the Text; Step 5: Filling the Gaps; Step 6: Signing or Speaking the Word; plus a wrap-up discussion of the six steps.
- **Module 6: Introduction to community review** — Reminds participants of the full CBBT process and the bridge analogy, then introduces basic community review with practice getting feedback on drafts.
  - _Activities:_ Activity 1: Cook skit — six chairs/objects represent process stages, with a "cook" serving "food" and asking taste-test questions mapped to TUAA, showing how drafts cycle back; Activity 2: Learning the community review process — a group plays its draft for a mock "community" who retell it so the team can spot unclear parts.
- **Module 7: Preparing to begin translation** — Reviews the CBBT ethos, what a good translation is, how to understand a passage, how to draft, and the CBBT process, and gets the team making concrete decisions on how they will begin drafting. Built around teach-back practice with debriefs.
  - _Activities:_ Activity 1: Accessing the technology and FIA materials; Activity 2: Practicing describing a good translation (TUAA); Activity 3: Practicing describing how to understand a passage well (five ways of learning); Activity 4: Practicing the 6 FIA steps of drafting; Activity 5: Practicing the CBBT process.

### Course 3: PERPETUATION — Continuing well in Bible translation

**Google Doc:** https://docs.google.com/document/d/1mfBAtorpmqMf5PWGH867Pb6zAPnuCex4YJ5OkABR4Ao/
**Overview:** The third and final CBBT course — an advanced quality-assurance check-in held about 3 months after Course 2 as a one-week event. Primarily for the SAE and SRB, with translators, team leaders, and community reviewers joining as relevant. Teams review and refine their processes, receive further QA equipping, and plan for the perpetual improvement of their translation. Highly participatory and customized to the teams' actual needs gathered beforehand.

**Modules:**

- **Module 1: Opening session** — Welcomes and orients participants to the course goals and schedule; invites updates on projects since Course 2. Uses Nehemiah 8 (Ezra reading the Law, Levites translating) told twice with devotional questions.
- **Module 2: Review of CBBT Circle** — Reviews the full CBBT process from internalizing through SRB approval plus each group's roles; the trainer assesses how much review is needed.
  - _Option 1 (Brief):_ teams re-demonstrate their current CBBT process twice (second time the trainer pauses to ask about adaptations/skipped steps); debrief.
  - _Option 2 (In-depth):_ full circle activity with 5 groups plus the team leader, passing a "Draft #1" around until the SRB blesses it.
- **Module 3: Review of Bridge and TUAA** — Reviews the "bridge" picture of translation and the 4 TUAA qualities; trainer assesses depth needed.
  - _Option 1 (Brief):_ recall and explain each TUAA quality in small groups; discuss how they ensure quality.
  - _Option 2 (In-depth):_ repeats the Course 2 bridge and TUAA activities (incl. Activity 1: The Bridge).
- **Module 4: Giving and receiving feedback** — Equips participants with a simple feedback model for all QA processes. Discusses culturally appropriate feedback via Ephesians 4:14-16, then introduces the "clear, kind, specific, helpful" formula; teams craft their own.
  - _Activity 1:_ the trainer draws a simple picture and improves it over 3–4 rounds using participants' feedback.
- **Module 5: Translation Brief Update** — An advanced check-in to review the team's own "Translation Project Planning & Guidelines" document and verify current processes produce a draft aligned with it; focuses on the "Translation Guidelines" part and key-term recording.
- **Module 6: Key Terms and Translation Style** — Equips SAEs and SRB with a framework for choosing renderings of key terms that fit their chosen translation style (literal vs. moderate meaning-based vs. simple/contemporary). Works through sample terms (denarius, Sabbath, centurion) across three levels of literalness.
  - _Option 1:_ with written documentation (rendering-choices chart/worksheet); _Option 2:_ oral/visual projects — designate three places in the room for the three style levels and move between them.
- **Module 7: Community Review** — Reminds participants of the Community Review process and practices forming good comprehension questions and a process for using feedback; involves community members not previously part of drafting. Two rounds (trainer models, then SAE/translators/team leader lead).
  - _Activities:_ Retell test; Asking comprehension questions (open-ended who/what/when/where/why, avoid yes/no); Practicing community feedback on a different draft.
- **Module 8: Detailed verification of the draft [for SAEs]** — SAEs observe and practice a detailed verification that a draft (which has completed the full 6-step FIA process incl. Community and Church Reviews) reflects the 4 qualities, using the Checking Questions. Two rounds (trainer models, then SAE leads); ends with the SAE recommending the passage to the SRB.
  - _Option 1:_ verifying quality with written resources; _Option 2:_ verifying quality in visual and oral projects (AVTT/SLTT recordings).
- **Module 9: Reviewing Scripture for use in church [SRB]** — The SRB observes and practices reviewing draft quality, confirming the process was followed, the draft reflects the 4 qualities and aligns with the brief, then gives final approval and authorizes church use. Two rounds; ends with a formal SRB approval statement.
  - _Option 1:_ reviewing quality in cluster (multi-language) projects; _Option 2:_ reviewing quality in single-language projects.
- **Module 10: Technology check-in** _(time as needed — possibly several hours or a separate session)_ — Ensures all participants are confident using their chosen technology tools (AVTT, SLTT, Render, etc.) and provides additional training; trainer should learn the team's tools in advance.
- **Module 11: Planning for the future of your translation** — Encourages teams to plan for continuous improvement and identify a stewardship team and process. Uses the story of the NIV's standing Committee on Bible Translation and the bridge analogy to motivate ongoing revision, since language is living and changing.
- **Module 12: Blessing ceremony — SRB** — Prepares the SRB to formally authorize and bless Scripture portions for church use, then holds a formal blessing ceremony. Shows historic videos of SRB/pastor-committee blessings (Papua New Guinea, Georgia).
  - _Part 1:_ preparing for the blessing ceremony (held earlier in the week); _Part 2:_ the formal blessing ceremony as part of a celebratory closing.
